ABOUT THE FILM

La Piscine , is a psychological drama set under the intense summer sun of the French Riviera. What begins as a peaceful holiday between friends slowly transforms into a tense and emotional confrontation shaped by desire, jealousy and hidden resentment.

Through elegant cinematography, minimal dialogue and a haunting atmosphere of luxury and silence, the film explores the fragility of human relationships beneath the surface of paradise.

DIRECTOR

JACQUES DERAY

YEAR

1969

FILMING LOCATION

SAINT TROPEZ, FRANCE

INSIDE THE EXHIBITION

Visitors will discover how the film was shot, examining the cinematography, lighting and visual compositions that created its iconic atmosphere of heat, silence and psychological tension. Through original film stills, archival material and audiovisual installations, the exhibition reveals how every frame was carefully constructed to reflect desire, intimacy and emotional unease.

The exhibition also presents a collection of vintage press catalogues, promotional posters, magazine covers and production documents that explore the cultural impact of La Piscine at the time of its release. Alongside these materials, visitors will encounter costume pieces and fashion references inspired by the film’s elegant Riviera aesthetic, showcasing the timeless style that became one of its most iconic elements.

Behind-the-scenes photography, rare production insights and visual installations dedicated to the cast offer a closer look into the making of the film, allowing visitors to step beyond the screen and into the atmosphere of 1960s European cinema.
